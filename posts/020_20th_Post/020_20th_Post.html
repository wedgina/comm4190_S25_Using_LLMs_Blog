Writing Creative Stories with Google Gemini

Not sure who remembers what madlibs are, but when I was younger my teachers used to have us doing them for funsies. They’re a story filled with a few blanks throughout the passage, of a story that’s supposed to be funny and random and you’re supposed to fill them in to have a funny story. We used to give random words based on what that blank required (adjective, verbs, noun, etc)—Wait a minute, I just realized that they did this to get us to also practice grammar at the same time. Since LLMs are not humans do they even get humor and creativity like we do? Or will they just choose random nonsense to fill in the blanks? Today I am going to be using Google Gemini to see how it will go about filling in those blanks.

Code 
import google.generativeai as genai
from IPython.display import Image, display, Markdown
from dotenv import load_dotenv
from PIL import Image
import os
_ = load_dotenv()

genai.configure()

model = genai.GenerativeModel(model_name="models/gemini-1.5-pro-latest")

response = model.generate_content(["Can you provide an explanation and evidence to support the question on if the removal of Affirmative Action and DEI is right and determine which side is stronger"])

def create_madlibs(image_path, model_name="models/gemini-1.5-pro-latest"):
    model = genai.GenerativeModel(model_name=model_name)
    image = Image.open(image_path)
    prompt = (
        "This is a Mad Libs story image with blanks. The goal is to have a completed funny and creative story, please return the full completed story."
    )
    response = model.generate_content(
        [prompt, image],
        generation_config=genai.types.GenerationConfig(temperature=0.9)
    )
    return response.text

Conclusion

I think overall I enjoyed the second story the most, I think it had the best elements of random and actually kept me the most intrigued. It also felt like it gave purposely random responses well and in a way that made a lot more sense. See the third story definitely felt like what my original question was asking, whether the responses would just feel like random or would it reflect more of the creativity humans have, and it definitely felt a lot more random. And the first story felt like it fit neither, it wasn’t that creative to me nor was it that random, which made it unsatisfactory to read. I feel like a nice way to be creative there, was when the story required a body part was to throw in some more, less- thought-of body parts or perhaps some internal organs. I think that Gemini at least is good at the basics of story generation but nothing that replicates the creativity, meaningfulness, and the genuineness of that of a human’s creativity.
